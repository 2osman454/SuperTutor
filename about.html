About me

Overview

As a proud graduate with a Bachelor of Science Honours (BSCH) degree in Biochemistry and Mathematical Science from the University of Guelph, I am an aspiring researcher with research interests in structural biology and bioinformatics.

Apart from family and schoolwork, I spend most of my free time tutoring high school students in Mathematics, Chemistry, and Physics.

Presented here is a snapshot of the development of my personal and professional competence, and why tutoring is important for any student.

Secondary School and Kakuma Refugee Camp

While spending most primary (elementary) and junior high school time as a shy kid, my interaction with classmates started in my senior high school years at Kakuma Refugee Secondary School (KRSS) and Pleasant View Secondary School (now Pleasant View Schools), Kenya.

After emerging as the third-best overall performer in KRSS and Turkana West District of Turkana County for the 2013 Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) exams, I was employed as an incentive teacher by the Lutheran World Federation (LWF) in January 2014 and byWindle Trust Kenya (WTK) the following year.

Incentive Teacher

Like other incentive workers who got similar incentive teaching positions, it was at KRSS that I earned the title of “Mwalimu,” a Swahili word for “teacher”.

As an incentive teacher in Kakuma Refugee Camp, I was more than happy to receive a monthly stipend of KSH7200 (about CAD80), one of the world's lowest wages as reported previously by Kakuma News Reflector (KANERE)

At KRSS, I taught Chemistry, Mathematics, and Physics. The development of my personal and professional competence began at KRSS. The more I explained the concepts, the better I understood the topics and gained more confidence.

My reward and source of motivation was not the monthly stipend but the compliment and results from my students. That meant and still means a lot to me.

The University of Guelph and Canada

In 2015, I was fortunate enough to be among a group of students sponsored by the World University Service of Canada (WUSC) to study at the University of Guelph. The Student Refugee Program (SRP), through WUSC, combines resettlement with opportunities for higher education and supports over 150 refugee students annually. Visit the WUSC website to volunter your time or donate.

During my undergraduate studies at the University of Guelph, I enjoyed explaining scientific concepts to my classmates and group members during group discussions. Consequently, the appreciation and compliments I received still keeps me moving and even so are the rewarding results of the beneficiaries of my service. Check out my reviews and recommendations!

Why Tutoring is Important

From personal experience, a classroom is one of the busiest places and not all students can thrive well in such an environment as many become distracted and lose their focus on the class exercise and homework. Every student in a given classroom learns differently and there is almost no access to individualized instruction.

It is always challenging for teachers and instructors to ensure that all students receive quality instruction no matter how hard they try to utilize the Universal Design for Learning (UDL) approach. Basically, the aim of UDL is to provide equity and access to all students and to ensure that every student’s goals and objectives are addressed.

As I kept teaching at KRSS and later help my friends and classmates during my undergraduate studies, I learned that any student is capable of solving any given problem but needs help to do so given not only the limited interaction with teachers and professors, large class sizes and multiple distractions, but also the socioeconomic barriers that impact the student's progress.

Students can benefit from one-on-one interaction as tutoring removes these barriers and fills the gaps left by the fast-paced learning environment. Do not hesitate to contact me for a FREE consultation.
